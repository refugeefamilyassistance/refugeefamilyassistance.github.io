What is a refugee?

A refugee is someone who has been forced to flee conflict or persecution and cannot return to their country without risking their life or freedoms. It is a legal term that carries with it certain protections that refugees are entitled to.

A refugee is different from an asylum seeker, a migrant worker, or an undocumented immigrant.


How do refugees get here?

Refugees undergo a series of 8 background checks and security clearances over the course of several months to several years. They are the most vetted immigrants to come to the United States.

Refugees are given full legal status upon arrival and are legally allowed to work in the U.S. They pay taxes and they also pay back all funds lent to them for resettlement to the U.S.

Over 20 years, refugees contribute more to the U.S economy than was given to them upon resettlement.


Executive Orders

Recent executive orders have deeply impacted the lives of refugees and the services available to serve them.

  2. A second executive order placed an immediate stop to all federally-funded assistance services that were promised to refugees who had recently arrived to the U.S. as they adjust to life in a new country.
